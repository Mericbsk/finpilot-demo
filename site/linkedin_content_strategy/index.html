    POST 5: Educational - RSI Explained¶

    📊 RSI Explained in 60 seconds (save this)

RSI = Relative Strength Index

One of the most useful indicators, yet most people use it wrong.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

𝗪𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗶𝘁 𝗺𝗲𝗮𝘀𝘂𝗿𝗲𝘀:
Momentum - is the stock overbought or oversold?

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝘀𝗰𝗮𝗹𝗲:
0 ←―――――――――――――――→ 100
     Oversold    Overbought

𝗧𝗿𝗮𝗱𝗶𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻𝗮𝗹 𝗿𝘂𝗹𝗲𝘀:
• RSI > 70 = Overbought (might drop)
• RSI < 30 = Oversold (might rise)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

𝗕𝗨𝗧 𝗵𝗲𝗿𝗲'𝘀 𝘄𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗺𝗼𝘀𝘁 𝗽𝗲𝗼𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝗴𝗲𝘁 𝘄𝗿𝗼𝗻𝗴:

❌ RSI > 70 ≠ "sell immediately"
In strong uptrends, RSI can stay above 70 for weeks.

✅ Better approach:
Look for RSI divergence + price action confirmation.

This is exactly what FinPilot's AI does automatically.
